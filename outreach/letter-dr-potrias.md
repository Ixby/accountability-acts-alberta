**Subject:** A drafted accountability package for Alberta — would value your read

Dear Dr. Potrias,

I hope you're well. I'm writing about a project that's grown out of the kinds of questions we've talked about in your classroom.

Over the past few months I've drafted what I think a serious provincial-level accountability package could look like for Alberta — two bills (one on how officials behave, one on how advantage flows through the system), with the design rationale, failure-mode analysis, and a written-up brief. It's released under Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike so any party can adopt it without paying the cost of being first, and it's deliberately structured to bind whoever holds power.

I'm reaching out because the package has a gap I cannot close from where I sit, and where your read would matter most.

The bill includes provisions touching the interface between Crown accountability machinery and Indigenous governance — a §21A carve-out for internal Indigenous governance, a §21A(7) carve-out for Indigenous-owned governance instruments (economic-development corporations and similar entities), an Indigenous Accountability Council under §21A(8), and a §32 staging provision that prevents the engagement-related parts of §21A from coming into force until the Minister tables an engagement report describing co-development with Treaty 6, Treaty 7, Treaty 8, the Métis Nation of Alberta, and the Métis Settlements General Council. I've adopted the UNDRIP free-prior-and-informed-consent standard as the engagement floor, notwithstanding that Alberta has not yet implemented UNDRIP.

I am a settler citizen. The drafting is the drafting of a settler. I have been honest about that in the brief — there is a section called *On Indigenous engagement* that names what I have tried to resolve preemptively from a settler vantage, and what only Indigenous engagement can determine. I do not have the standing to do the engagement that the Crown owes, and I do not pretend to. What I can do is reach out, in personal capacity, to people whose read I trust before any party touches the document.

Three things I would value from you, if any are of interest:

1. **Your read on the *On Indigenous engagement* section** as it stands — whether the framing of the asymmetry between the two governance traditions is honest, whether the resolutions I've attempted in §21A are sensible starting points or carry blind spots a careful reader would catch, and whether the package as a whole reads as something an Indigenous government would engage with in good faith or as something they would dismiss as another settler accountability instrument.
2. **Pointers to people, organizations, or scholarly work** I should be reading or reaching out to next. I have tried to identify the obvious starting points (the treaty representative bodies, the MNA, MSGC, the leading Indigenous-law scholars) but I am sure I am missing things only someone closer to the field would catch.
3. **Any willingness to be cited or named** in the package's *About the drafter* section as someone the drafter consulted, on whatever terms you would prefer — including not at all. I do not want to imply endorsement you have not given. I do want to be honest about who I have spoken with.

The package is at github.com/Ixby/accountability-acts-alberta and the PDF of the full brief is in the repo. I am happy to send specific sections rather than the whole document, or to walk through any of it with you over coffee or whatever else works for your schedule.

There is no timeline pressure. The package isn't going anywhere I haven't told it to, and I would rather get the engagement right than get it fast.

Thank you for your time on this — and for the conversations that helped shape the questions in the first place.
